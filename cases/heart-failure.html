30-Day Readmission Risk Scoring

Problem Statement

Hospital readmissions within 30 days for heart failure patients are a critical indicator of post-discharge care quality and incur massive healthcare overheads. Predicting high-risk patients requires models that are not only accurate but also provide clear causal explanations to clinicians.

Bayesian Network DAG & Modeling

Rather than relying solely on black-box neural networks, we constructed a probabilistic Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) mapping clinical variables (Serum Creatinine, Ejection Fraction, Age, Diabetes, High Blood Pressure):

  • Bayesian Network Structure: Encoded conditional independence relations among physiological metrics.
  • Probabilistic Inference: Used Variable Elimination to calculate posterior readmission probabilities given partial clinical evidence.
  • Naive Bayes Baseline: Provided lightweight risk scoring with fast inference times for real-time triage.
# Bayesian Network Modeling Excerpt (Python / Pgmpy) from pgmpy.models import BayesianNetwork from pgmpy.estimators import MaximumLikelihoodEstimator from pgmpy.inference import VariableElimination # Define causal DAG structure model = BayesianNetwork([ ('Age', 'HighBloodPressure'), ('HighBloodPressure', 'EjectionFraction'), ('SerumCreatinine', 'Readmission30Days'), ('EjectionFraction', 'Readmission30Days') ]) model.fit(data, estimator=MaximumLikelihoodEstimator) infer = VariableElimination(model) # Query posterior probability given patient evidence result = infer.query(variables=['Readmission30Days'], evidence={'EjectionFraction': '<30%', 'SerumCreatinine': '>1.5'}) print(result)

Key Outcomes

Constructed an interpretable clinical risk model allowing physicians to see the exact conditional probabilities driving a patient's readmission risk score.
